<p><p>The lung cancer diagnosis and therapeutics market is categorized into Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) and Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC). NSCLC, which accounts for the majority of lung cancer cases, is characterized by slower growth and encompasses subtypes like adenocarcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma. SCLC, in contrast, grows more rapidly and is often associated with a history of smoking. Diagnosis typically involves imaging and biopsies, while therapeutics include surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapies, and immunotherapy tailored to cancer type and stage.</p></p>
