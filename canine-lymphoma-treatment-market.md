<p><p>The canine lymphoma treatment market encompasses several drug types, each targeting specific aspects of the disease. Alkylating agents work by damaging DNA in cancer cells, leading to cell death. Corticosteroids reduce inflammation and suppress the immune response, aiding in symptomatic relief. Purine analogs disrupt nucleic acid synthesis, hindering cancer cell proliferation. Antimetabolites mimic natural substances, interfering with cell division. Immune modulators enhance the dog's immune response against cancer. "Others" may include novel therapies or supportive treatments that complement existing options.</p></p>
