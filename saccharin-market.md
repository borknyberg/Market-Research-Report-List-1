<p><p>The saccharin market is divided into two main types: insoluble and soluble saccharin. Insoluble saccharin is primarily used in applications like tablets and granules, where it remains undissolved, providing concentrated sweetness without altering texture. In contrast, soluble saccharin dissolves easily in liquids, making it ideal for beverages and syrups. Both types serve various industries, including food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and personal care, catering to specific consumer preferences for sweetness and dietary requirements.</p></p>
