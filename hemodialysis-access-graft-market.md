<p><p>The hemodialysis access graft market consists of three main types: biological grafts, synthetic grafts, and biosynthetic grafts. Biological grafts are derived from natural tissues, providing biocompatibility and reduced risk of infection. Synthetic grafts, made from materials like polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), offer durability and ease of use but may present a higher risk of complications. Biosynthetic grafts combine synthetic materials with biological properties, aiming to enhance performance and minimize adverse effects. Each type serves to establish vascular access for effective hemodialysis treatment.</p></p>
