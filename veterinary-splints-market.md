<p><p>The veterinary splints market is divided into inflatable and non-inflatable types. Inflatable splints offer adjustable support and flexibility, allowing for varying degrees of pressure and stabilization, suitable for soft tissue injuries and post-operative recovery. Non-inflatable splints, on the other hand, provide rigid support for fractures and structural injuries, ensuring immobilization of the affected area. Both types are essential for effective treatment in veterinary practices, catering to different injury requirements and enhancing animal comfort during recovery.</p></p>
