Laminating involves using wax to enhance the barrier properties of packaging materials, improving moisture resistance and shelf life for food products. Coating refers to applying wax on food surfaces to create a protective layer that prevents spoilage and maintains appearance. Hardening involves the use of wax in various food formulations to achieve desired texture and stability, making it ideal for confections and baked goods.</p></p>
