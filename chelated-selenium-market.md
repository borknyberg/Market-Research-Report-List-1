<p><p>Chelated selenium is categorized into two primary market types: organic and inorganic. Organic chelated selenium involves selenium molecules bonded to organic compounds, enhancing bioavailability and absorption in living organisms. It is often used in dietary supplements and functional foods. In contrast, inorganic chelated selenium consists of selenium combined with inorganic salts, typically employed in animal feed and fertilizers. Both types serve specific applications, with organic choices favored for nutritional purposes and inorganic forms utilized for agricultural enhancements.</p></p>
