Baseband coaxial cable is designed for short-distance communication, transmitting digital signals without modulation, making it ideal for local area networks (LANs) and video applications. In contrast, broadband coaxial cable accommodates a wide range of frequencies, enabling high-speed data transfer over longer distances, typically utilized for cable television and internet services. Both types are essential for modern telecommunications infrastructure.</p></p>
