<p><p>Sourdough markets are categorized into three types based on production methods and characteristics. Type I Sourdough involves traditional fermentation with a high percentage of sourdough culture, resulting in a distinct tangy flavor. Type II Sourdough balances commercial yeast with sourdough, offering a milder taste and quicker production times. Type III Sourdough utilizes primarily commercial yeast with minimal sourdough culture, providing a less pronounced sour flavor and optimized for mass production. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and baking applications.</p></p>
